ROUTINE ORDER

Layering Skincare

https://hudabeauty.com/us/en_US/blog-how-to-layer-your-skincare-39623.html

Before we dive into april's routine, we need to know that the order of the products you use in your routine are very important. This is because the order in which you use/layer your products allows for each product to work efficiently in terms of its consistency, benefits and barrier/shielding properties.

Note: Not all steps have to be followed! This is merely just a guide.

Note: Also, these products may work for me but they may not work for you! Please conduct your own research for your own skincare safety


Step 1. Cleanser

Before you apply anything to your precious face, you must remove any dirt or makeup that is left over from either your beauty sleep or your busy day. This can include cleansing products such as water-based cleansers, oil cleansers, makeup wipes, etc.

(To see specific recommendations of this product, visit my 'Recommendations' page in the 'Cleansers' section.)


Step 2. Toner

After washing away those impurities, you might want to rebalance the ph of your skin, cleanse anything you may have missed in the previous step or to just prepare your skin for the rest of your routine. Depending on your toner, it can help achieve any of these benefits. It isn't a necessary step as cleansing the skin can be enough, a majority of the time, but it is nice to have.

(To see specific recommendations of this product, visit my 'Recommendations' page in the 'Toners' section.)


Step 2.5. Essence

If you're feeling extra (and have enough spare change in that student budget of yours), it's not a step that's needed but it can be beneficial to use an essence. An essence is like a toner with a thicker consistency that's primary job is to provide hydration and healing. Personally I live by using an essence, especially the COSRX Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence.


Step 3. Serum

If you have specific skin concerns that you would like to tackle, you can find a serum that matches what you're looking to fix/improve and apply it at this stage of the routine. This is so that it can be easily absorbed by the skin after the previous steps as it has the most lightweight, thin formula than the others.


Step 4. Eye Cream

Dark circles and eye bags can be a nuissance so now's the time to fight back against them! These usually small creams pack a punch despite being 20ml at most. They're perfect for applying before other creams as they are much thinner and can be absorbed into the skin faster without any bulkier creams getting in its way.

Note: It is recommended that if your eye cream contains retinol, you can apply it after your moisturiser as it will act as an extra barrier for the eye cream to go through, reducing potential irritation.


Step 5. Face Cream

Face creams include products such as moisturiser, day creams, night creams, anti-aging creams, etc. These are often second to last or the very last step as they can be the thickest in consistency and help lock in all the products you have used before this. In my opinion, if there could only be three items you could use in your routine, it would be a cleanser, a moisturiser and our last product: a sun cream.

(To see specific recommendations of this product, visit my 'Recommendations' page in the 'Face Creams' section.)


Step 5.5. Facial Oil

Personally, I am not a fan of facial oils as my skin is already oily enough (even though it has been debunked that facial oils in fact do not make oily skin worse), they can benefit others in terms of repairing your skin barrier and providing nutrients. It can be the heaviest out of all the products mentioned but it helps as an extra shield against the environment's skin threats. I have tried facial oils before when my skin wasn't as oily as it is now and I used to love the Trilogy Certified Organic Rosehip Oil.


Step 6. Sun Cream

Probably one of the most emphasized products by dermatologists and skincare experts alike (and for good reasons) the final step of your routine must include sun cream (unless your moisturiser has a good amount of spf). It provides the most important barrier of protection in your arsenal of products which is why it must be applied last so that it's shielding properties can be used to it's fullest potential against the threat of skin cancer and UV rays.
