Recommendations

Welcome to my recommendations page where I'll share, in my opinion and from my own research and experience, the skincare products that best suit your skin concerns!

Whether you have dry, oily, or combination skin, you can find a product that can best help you from a range of the main types of skincare products: cleansers, toners, and, face creams.


Skin Types

There are 6 main skin types:

♥ Oily Skin:

This is my primary skin type. This is the type of skin where people often mistake your shininess for a natural glow, while in reality, it is caused by the excess production of sebum or oil by the skin.

♥ Dry Skin:

Dry skin is characterized by a lack of moisture in the skin, which can cause itching, flaking, and cracking. People with this type of skin need to focus their skincare routine on hydrating and retaining moisture.

♥ Acne Skin:

This is a skin type that is relevant to (but not limited to) most teenagers and young adults. The appearance of pimples, blemishes, whiteheads and, blackheads are common here due to excess sebum and clogged pores.

♥ Combination Skin:

Just as the name suggests, this is a blend of both dry and oily skin which can make it a bit challenging to manage. Since skincare products are usually formulated based on specific skin types, finding the right products for combination skin can be tricky.

♥ Normal Skin:

Normal skin types are the opposite of the previous skin type. They are neither oily nor dry and tend to have well-balanced skin. People with normal skin usually have nothing to worry about, as they tend to have little to no acne, fine lines, or wrinkles. Most, if not all, products can be used on normal skin (provided that there are no allergies), so I don't have any specific recommendations for this skin type.

♥ Sensitive Skin:

Sensitive skin is easily irritated by various environmental factors, such as temperature changes, certain chemicals, and allergens. Those who have this skin type must be careful when using a new product and should conduct enough research to find out if that product is suitable for them.

Now that we have enough knowledge about the different skin types, let's explore the different products that are suggested for each.


1. Cleansers

Just as there are different skin types, there are different kinds of cleansers to accomodate them.


i. La Roche-Posay Effaclar Purifying Cleansing Gel

La Roche-Posay Effaclar Purifying Cleansing Gel

This was the second cleanser I ever bought in my life, and for good reasons. It's affordable for it's size and it helped control my oily/acne-prone skin.


ii. Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser

Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser

If you have read my night routine, you might recognise this cleanser as I still use it to this day. To summarize, its simple ingredients make it suitable for sensitive skin, and it doesn't leave that dry feeling that cleansers tend to leave you with.


iii. Youth To The People Superfood Cleanser

Youth To The People Superfood Cleanser

Referred to as a 'Cult Classic', this powerful cleanser contains many ideal ingredients such as vitamins C, E and K and a smoothie-like blend of kale, green tea and spinach. It is recommended for combination skin due to its lightweight yet moisturising formula.


2. Toners

Toners are very versatile products that are beneficial for targetting certain skin types.


i. Cosrx AHA/BHA Clarifying Treatment Toner

Cosrx AHA/BHA Clarifying Treatment Toner

Again, if you have read my night routine, you might recognise this toner. It is very suitable for oily, acne prone skin as it reduces the excess sebum produced by your skin but doesn't leave it too dry. It also helps to clear your pores and to minimise whiteheads and blackheads.


ii. LANEIGE Cream Skin Toner & Moisturizer

LANEIGE Cream Skin Toner & Moisturizer

Technically, this is both a toner and a moisturiser but the formula is so lightweight that it's essentially a glorified toner. Its milky texture is great for dry skin, and its ingredients, like white-leaf tea water, are highly hydrating.


iii. Pyunkang Yul Essence Toner

Pyunkang Yul Essence Toner

This toner originates from Korea and has incredible ingredients such as arginine, which repairs and moisturises the skin. It absorbs quickly and helps firm your face, making it perfect for combination skin.


iv. ETUDE HOUSE SoonJung PH 5.5 Relief Toner

ETUDE HOUSE SoonJung PH 5.5 Relief Toner

The SoonJung PH 5.5 Relief Toner was designed with sensitive skin types in mind. It is free of irritants such as fragrance and artificial colours, and helps to hydrate and cool down the skin.


3. Face Creams

Usually, the last step of a skincare routine (if you exclude sunscreen), face creams are essential for locking in the previous layers of products in your routine and providing an extra boost of hydration.


i. La Roche-Posay Effaclar MAT

La Roche-Posay Effaclar MAT

This matte moisturiser contains an oil-free formula that targets oily skin. By controlling the sebum in your skin, it effectively aids in soothing and clearing acne.


ii. COSRX Advanced Snail 92 All in One Cream

COSRX Advanced Snail 92 All in One Cream

It shouldn't come as a shock to anyone that I am, once again, recommending this powerhouse cream due to its main ingredient of snail mucin. I use it myself in my night routine and find that it is extremely hydrating, leaving my skin soft and smooth.


iii. The Ordinary Natural Moisturizing Factors + HA

The Ordinary Natural Moisturizing Factors + HA

Another product that I proudly use, but this time in my morning routine, is formulated with hyaluronic acid to provide hydration and amino acids to help maintain your skin's barrier. This makes it perfect for combination skin.


iv. iUnik Centella Calming Gel Cream

iUnik Centella Calming Gel Cream

This refreshing gel cream relieves sensitive skin, along with acne-prone skin! It contains Centella Asiatica, an ingredient known to calm redness and irritation.
