Step 1. Cleanser

A nighttime cleanse is more important than a morning cleanse. Why? Because of the makeup you may still have on or all the dirt that potentially built up on your skin during the day. But you can't just cleanse once! You should do a double cleanse to make sure everything has been washed away.

A double cleanse is where you first wash your face with an oil-based product which will help break down your makeup, excess sebum (oil), and the residue of sunscreen that's still lingering on your face. Then you follow up with a water-based cleanser which cleans out impurities that are water-soluble like dirt and sweat.


First Cleanse: Oil

In order to use an oil cleanser, you have to massage it onto a dry face with dry hands. Once it is fully incorporated into the skin, you wet your hands and massage your face again to emulsify the oil. This means that the oil cleanser will start to turn milky white which helps get rid of the oily feeling it gives. Then wet your hands even more to fully wash off the oil.

Our first and only Japanese entry, this oil cleanser lives up to its name - it's indeed speedy. It makes the tedious task of oil cleansing a much quicker task. Second Cleanse: Water-based

In general, most cleansers are water-based so there is no need to go out of your way to find one. Just like my morning moisturiser, I have been using this basic, reliable product for a long time:


Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser

Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser

Compared to the other items on this list, this cleanser may seem unremarkable. However, its simplicity makes it highly suitable for sensitive skin. Don't let its lack of complexity fool you, though - it's just as effective as the rest! In fact, sometimes simplicity in skincare can be a real blessing, and this cleanser is a prime example of that.


Step 2. Toner

If you couldn't tell so far, during the night, I prefer to keep my routine simple. Because of this, to address my skin concerns I use exfoliating toners which help balance your skin's pH and exfoliate its surface. However, because they can contain such potent ingredients, I mostly use them every second day or twice a week. Again, as someone who suffers from hyperpigmentation, these toners really help battle that issue.


Cosrx AHA/BHA Clarifying Treatment Toner

Cosrx AHA/BHA Clarifying Treatment Toner

Designed for those new to chemical exfoliation, this product offers a gentle introduction to AHAs and BHAs. What is AHA or BHA you may ask? They are hydroxy acids that are used to treat various skin conditions like acne. The toner is conveniently packaged in a spray bottle, making application a breeze. It's particularly well-suited for those with sensitive skin, as the acids work gradually and without the typical tingling sensation often associated with exfoliating toners.
